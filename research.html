  • Baroclinic Turbulence over Continental Slopes

    • Untitled Document


    Continental slopes constitute much of the steepest part of the ocean bed. Owing to the large topographic PV gradient, large-scale flows over continental slopes tend to follow the isobaths. However, these flows are commonly associated with cross-stream velocity shear and buoyancy gradient, leading to barotropic and baroclinic instabilities, from which mesoscale eddies develop. This study focuses on the interaction between mesoscale eddies and large-scale flows over continental slopes and the cross-slope transfer driven by mesoscale eddies.
