Yan Wang
Assistant Professor

My work has been focusing on transport and mixing processes in the ocean. I have adopted ideas from both geophysical fuid dynamics and applied mathematics to study baroclinic turbulence over continental slopes and long range transport by coherent Lagrangian vortices. My approaches cover theory, numerical modeling, and observations.

I obtained my B.Sc. Degree in Marine Science from Ocean University of China in June 2010 and Ph.D. Degree in Applied Marine Physics from the Rosenstiel School of Marine and Atmospheric Science, University of Miami in December 2015. Prior to joining HKUST, I was a postdoc scholar at the Deparment of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences, University of California, Los Angeles from January 2016 through August 2018.
